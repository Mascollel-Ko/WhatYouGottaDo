# v0.4.1.10.4 ProgramBuilder Quality Optimization Release Notes

## Baseline

- Started from: `v0.4.1.10.3`
- Branch: `fix/program-builder-quality-optimization-v041104`
- Release version: `versionName = "v0.4.1.10.4"`, `versionCode = 401014`

## Reproduction Scenario

- Goal: badminton support strength training
- Duration: 4 weeks
- Weekly days: 5
- Daily minutes: 45
- Badminton:strength ratio: 60:40
- Equipment: all available equipment selected, including barbell, dumbbell, machine, cable, kettlebell, bodyweight, and app-defined extras
- Week 1 days example: Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday, Sunday

## Problems Fixed

- All-equipment plans could still underuse barbell, dumbbell, machine, and cable strength anchors because scoring and selection favored functional transfer candidates too strongly.
- Badminton-support 60:40 plans could drift toward transfer-only sessions instead of preserving normal strength anchors.
- 45-minute sessions could look too sparse when useful candidates were available.
- Weekly plans could repeat the same stable key, movement family, muscle group, or transfer purpose too often.
- Five-day weeks needed hard, medium, and light day rhythm without flattening all sessions into the same intensity.
- The builder needed internal weekly/program-wide quality evaluation and bounded repair before exposing the generated skeleton.
- The UI needed immediate progress feedback while generation, evaluation, and optimization run in the background.

## Problems Not Changed

- `6-corner shadow footwork` remains an allowed badminton-transfer exercise.
- Day-chip navigation and weekday selector confusion are intentionally deferred.
- Equipment hard gates remain active; this release fixes all-equipment ranking/composition behavior rather than allowing unselected equipment.

## File Responsibility Map

- `ProgramCandidateInventory`: builds candidate inventory while preserving equipment and user-exclusion hard gates.
- `ProgramSlotCandidateQuery`: keeps slot query tracing, relaxation, and adaptive pool behavior from v0.4.1.10.3.
- `ProgramScoringPolicy`: ranks candidates and applies bounded loaded-strength and composition preferences.
- `ProgramCompositionPolicy`: owns badminton-support strength-anchor composition preference and warnings.
- `ProgramSessionDensityPolicy`: owns target exercise count and useful minimum density by session length.
- `ProgramVarietyPolicy`: owns distribution warnings and works with selection history to avoid repeated collapse.
- `ProgramSelectionHistory`: tracks stable key, family, role, transfer, and intensity history during selection.
- `ProgramDayIntensityPolicy`: classifies hard/medium/light day intensity and flags fatigue clustering.
- `ProgramEvaluationModels`: defines weekly/program evaluation scores, issue types, severity, and optimization summaries.
- `ProgramEvaluationPolicy`: evaluates generated skeleton quality for repair decisions and diagnostics.
- `ProgramRepairPolicy`: applies bounded, conservative repair decisions to sparse sessions.
- `ProgramOptimizationPolicy`: manages evaluation -> repair -> re-evaluation loops with max-iteration and improvement guards.
- `ProgramOptimizationTrace`: records optimization iterations and user-facing adjustment summaries.
- `ProgramBuildProgressState`: models idle/running/completed/failed auto-generation progress.
- `TrainingViewModel`: exposes progress state and runs auto generation off the UI thread.
- `PlanScreen` / `PlanEditorControls`: displays progress, disables unsafe duplicate actions, and shows a short adjustment summary.

## Evaluation Engine

- Daily, weekly, and program-wide signals are considered.
- Weekly imbalance is allowed when later weeks compensate.
- Repeated program-wide imbalance is penalized more strongly than one-week variation.
- The engine checks fatigue, strength anchors, badminton transfer spread, session density, intensity distribution, loaded-strength utilization, and overall balance.
- Evaluation details stay internal by default; the UI shows only a short optimization summary.

## Repair / Optimization Loop

- Maximum iterations: 3.
- A candidate repair is accepted only when the evaluation score improves meaningfully or issues are reduced.
- Repairs preserve equipment restrictions, user exclusions, time limits, and the badminton:strength intent.
- Current repairs are conservative and focus on sparse-session improvement rather than broad exercise swaps.
- If repair does not improve the result, the previous skeleton is retained.

## Progress UI

- Auto skeleton generation now emits staged running states.
- Heavy generation and optimization work is dispatched off the UI thread.
- The UI shows a progress bar, "잠시만 기다려 주세요", and a current-stage message.
- Auto-generation and save actions are disabled while generation is running.
- Completion shows the generated skeleton plus a short automatic adjustment summary.
- Failure shows a user-facing retry path.

## Remaining Risks

- Evaluation thresholds are intentionally soft and may need calibration from real generated samples.
- Progress uses staged updates, not exact unit-of-work percentages.
